Datasets

All the datasets used for analysis are Genome in a Bottle Consortium samples of the human reference genome. Genome in a Bottle is a public–private–academic consortium under the patronage of the National Institute of Standards and Technology. Its primary focus is on developing reference materials and standards that would lead to using a translated human genome in clinical practice. The already assembled data are widely used to develop and improve tools and technologies in bioinformatics, as well as for research purposes.  GIAB currently contains genomes of multiple individuals. For analysis, this thesis used reference samples of an individual identified as HG002, also known as the son of the Ashkenazim trio.
